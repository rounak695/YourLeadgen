# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

At YourLeadGen, we believe that the best software is built by communities that are diverse, welcoming, and inclusive. We pledge to make participation in our project and our community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We are committed to acting and interacting in ways that contribute to an open, healthy, and highly professional engineering culture.

## Our Standards

**Behaviors that contribute to a positive, professional environment include:**

* **Technical Empathy:** Understanding that every contributor comes with a different level of experience. We provide constructive, actionable, and kind feedback during code reviews.
* **Respectful Discourse:** Engaging in technical debates with respect. Focus on the code, the architecture, and the outcome—never the person.
* **Graceful Acceptance:** Accepting responsibility for mistakes, apologizing to those affected, and treating failures as learning opportunities.
* **Community First:** Prioritizing what is best for the overall project and its users over individual preferences.

**Behaviors that are strictly unacceptable include:**

* The use of sexualized language or imagery, and sexual attention or advances of any kind.
* Trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks.
* Public or private harassment.
* Publishing others' private information, such as a physical or email address, without their explicit permission.
* Any conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional engineering environment.

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing our standards of acceptable behavior. Maintainers reserve the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, and other contributions that are not aligned with this Code of Conduct.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies both within project spaces and in public spaces when an individual is officially representing the project or its community. 

## Enforcement

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, please report it to the core maintainer team at **rounakpaul881@gmail.com**. All complaints will be reviewed and investigated promptly, fairly, and with strict confidentiality.

We are here to build great software together. Let's treat each other with respect.
